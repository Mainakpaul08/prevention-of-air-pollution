Understanding Air Pollution

Air pollution involves harmful substances that enter the atmosphere, posing risks to health and the environment. It arises from both natural sources and human activities, significantly impacting urban areas.

What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful or excessive quantities of substances in the air, including gases, particulates, and biological molecules that can be detrimental to human health and the planet.

Causes of Air Pollution

  • Vehicle Emissions: The burning of gasoline and diesel in vehicles releases pollutants.
  • Industrial Emissions: Factories and power plants emit chemicals and particulates into the air.
  • Agricultural Activities: Livestock production and fertilizer use release methane and ammonia.
  • Burning of Fossil Fuels: Coal and oil burning in homes and factories contributes to smog and greenhouse gases.

Effects of Air Pollution

  • Respiratory Issues: Inhalation of pollutants can lead to asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory diseases.
  • Environmental Damage: Pollutants harm ecosystems, causing acid rain and threatening biodiversity.
  • Climate Change: Greenhouse gases trap heat, leading to global warming and extreme weather.
  • Reduced Visibility: Smog and haze affect visibility, impacting transportation and daily life.
